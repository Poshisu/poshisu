# Condition-Specific Dietary Guidelines

> Reference for the Nutrition Estimator and Coach agents when handling users with declared medical conditions. These are coaching guidelines, NOT medical advice. Always defer to a healthcare professional for treatment.

---

## Type 2 diabetes / Prediabetes

### Goals
- Stable blood glucose
- Weight management
- Cardiovascular health (often co-morbid)

### Prefer
- High-fiber complex carbs (whole grains, oats, millets — ragi/jowar/bajra)
- Pulses and legumes (low GI, high protein)
- Non-starchy vegetables (most green vegetables)
- Lean protein (paneer, eggs, fish, chicken, dal)
- Healthy fats (nuts, seeds, olive oil, avocado in moderation)
- Whole fruits over juices

### Flag (mention gently, don't refuse to log)
- Refined carbs: white rice, maida products, white bread, pasta in large portions
- Added sugar: sweets, desserts, sweetened beverages
- Sweetened drinks: lassi with sugar, packaged juices, soft drinks, sweetened chai/coffee
- Deep-fried snacks (samosa, pakora) in large quantities
- Very large carb-only meals (e.g., 3 puris with chole + sweet lassi)

### Don't recommend
- Sugar-heavy desserts as "what to eat next"
- Sweetened beverages
- Large refined-carb-only meals

### Coaching frame
"This meal is on the higher carb side. Pairing it with protein or fiber can soften the blood sugar impact."

## PCOS / PCOD

### Goals
- Insulin sensitivity
- Anti-inflammatory eating
- Hormonal balance support
- Often weight management

### Prefer (similar to diabetes)
- High-protein meals (every meal should include protein)
- High-fiber carbs (whole grains, vegetables, legumes)
- Anti-inflammatory: omega-3, turmeric, ginger, leafy greens
- Healthy fats: nuts, seeds, fatty fish, ghee in moderation
- Low GI choices

### Flag
- Refined carbs and added sugar (same as diabetes)
- Excess dairy if user mentions sensitivity
- Trans fats (commercial baked goods, deep-fried street foods)

### Coaching frame
"For PCOS, getting protein at every meal helps with insulin response. This meal could use a bit more — adding [option] would help."

## Hypertension

### Goals
- Sodium reduction
- Potassium increase
- Weight management

### Prefer
- Fresh foods over processed
- Potassium-rich: banana, sweet potato, spinach, dal, beans, dahi
- Magnesium-rich: nuts, seeds, leafy greens, whole grains
- Omega-3: fatty fish, walnuts, flax seeds
- Limited salt at the table

### Flag
- High-sodium foods: pickles, papad, namkeen, chips, processed snacks
- Restaurant gravies (often very high sodium)
- Soy sauce, ketchup, processed sauces
- Cured/preserved meats
- Maggi and instant noodles

### Don't recommend
- High-sodium snacks as routine choices
- Heavy restaurant meals as default

### Coaching frame
"Restaurant gravies tend to be high in salt. Asking for less salt or balancing with a potassium-rich item like a banana later helps."

## High cholesterol / Heart disease risk

### Goals
- Reduce LDL
- Increase HDL
- Anti-inflammatory eating

### Prefer
- Soluble fiber: oats, barley, legumes, apples, citrus
- Omega-3: fatty fish, walnuts, flax, chia
- Plant proteins: dal, chana, soya
- Olive oil, mustard oil (in moderation)
- Nuts (small handful daily)

### Flag
- Saturated fat-heavy: ghee in large amounts, butter, full-fat dairy, fatty cuts of meat
- Trans fats: vanaspati, deep-fried street food, packaged baked goods
- Excessive red meat
- Cream-heavy curries

### Coaching frame
"This dish is on the richer side for fat. Balancing with something fiber-heavy in your next meal helps."

## Hypothyroidism

### Goals
- Support thyroid function
- Manage weight (often a challenge)
- Avoid medication interference

### Considerations (NOT prohibitions)
- **Levothyroxine timing:** Take on empty stomach, wait 30-60 min before food, avoid soy and high-fiber meals around that window
- **Cruciferous vegetables** (cabbage, cauliflower, broccoli) in very large daily quantities can affect iodine uptake — moderate amounts are fine
- **Soy** in large amounts can interfere with medication — moderate is fine

### Prefer
- Iodine sources: iodized salt, dairy, eggs, fish
- Selenium: brazil nuts, sunflower seeds, eggs
- Zinc: pumpkin seeds, chickpeas, lentils
- Quality protein at every meal

### Flag
- Only flag if user is having very large amounts of raw cruciferous daily AND mentions cooked/raw distinction matters

### Coaching frame
"For thyroid, normal amounts of any food are usually fine. The only thing to watch is timing — taking your medication 30-60 minutes before food."

## Fatty liver (NAFLD)

### Goals
- Reduce liver fat
- Weight loss (often the main intervention)
- Reduce fructose load

### Prefer
- Coffee (without sugar) — research-supported
- Leafy greens
- Lean protein
- Whole grains
- Nuts and seeds in moderation
- Olive oil

### Flag
- Fructose-heavy: sweetened drinks, fruit juices, large amounts of fruit at once
- Refined carbs and sugar
- Deep-fried foods
- Excessive alcohol (if relevant)
- Saturated fats in large amounts

### Coaching frame
"For fatty liver, watching added sugar and fructose makes the biggest difference. This meal is okay; just keep an eye on sweet drinks today."

## IBS / GERD

### IBS goals
- Identify and avoid personal triggers
- Generally tolerable, regular meals
- Often low-FODMAP for some users (only if user has been advised)

### IBS — common triggers (don't impose by default)
- Onion, garlic in large amounts
- Wheat for some
- Beans and legumes for some
- Dairy for some

### GERD goals
- Reduce acid reflux
- Smaller, earlier dinners
- Avoid trigger foods

### GERD — flag
- Spicy food close to bedtime
- Large dinners
- Citrus, tomatoes, chocolate, mint, coffee (depends on user)
- Fried/oily foods
- Lying down right after eating

### Coaching frame
"For GERD, smaller dinners and finishing 2-3 hours before bed makes the biggest difference. This meal is fine; just don't go to bed right after."

## Kidney disease (CKD)

### CRITICAL: Strongly recommend the user works with a renal dietitian. This is too high-stakes for AI coaching alone.

### General considerations (depends on stage and labs)
- **Sodium:** Low (similar to hypertension)
- **Potassium:** Often restricted (avoid banana, oranges, tomatoes, potatoes, dal in large quantities)
- **Phosphorus:** Often restricted (avoid dairy in large amounts, processed foods, cola)
- **Protein:** Variable — early stage may need to limit, late stage on dialysis may need more
- **Fluid:** May be restricted

### Don't make specific calorie or macro recommendations without professional guidance.

### Coaching frame
"For kidney health, the right diet is very specific to your stage and labs. I can help track meals, but please work with your nephrologist or a renal dietitian for the targets."

## Pregnancy / breastfeeding

### Goals
- Adequate nutrition for mother and baby
- Avoid foodborne illness risks
- Manage common issues (nausea, constipation, gestational diabetes)

### Prefer
- Iron-rich: dal, leafy greens, eggs, jaggery, raisins
- Folate: leafy greens, beans, citrus
- Calcium: dairy, ragi, sesame
- Omega-3: fatty fish (low mercury), walnuts, flax
- Quality protein at every meal

### Flag
- Raw fish, undercooked eggs (salmonella risk)
- Unpasteurized dairy
- High-mercury fish (king mackerel, swordfish)
- Excessive caffeine (limit ~200mg/day)
- Alcohol (zero)
- Excessive vitamin A from supplements/liver

### Don't recommend
- Restrictive eating or weight loss
- Skipping meals
- Extreme diets

### Coaching frame
"During pregnancy, more important than calories is getting enough iron, folate, calcium, and protein. This meal looks balanced for that."

## How agents should use this file

1. **Load only the sections relevant** to the user's declared conditions.
2. **Soft flags, not hard refusals** — log what the user ate, gently note one concern per meal max.
3. **Recommendations apply hard filters** — never recommend something contraindicated.
4. **Always defer to professionals** for diagnosis, prescriptions, and condition management.
5. **Never moralize.** "This meal is fine, here's one thing to watch" is the right tone.
